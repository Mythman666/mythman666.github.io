Hrothgar

Grendel is one of the three monsters Beowulf fights. His character is ambiguous. Although he has many animal attributes and grotesque appearances, he seems to be guided by vague human emotions and impulses, and he shows more of an inner life than people think. Banished to the marshes beyond the boundaries of human society, Grendel is an outcast who seems eager to recover. The poet suggests that Grendel's invasion of the Danes was hidden by loneliness and jealousy.
